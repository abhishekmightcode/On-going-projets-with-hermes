# Bangalore Solar Market Overview
**Source:** Google Maps listings + company websites + May 2026 research

---

## Market Size & Growth

- **Karnataka** is among top 5 Indian states for rooftop solar installations
- **Bangalore** accounts for ~60-70% of Karnataka's rooftop solar demand (commercial + residential)
- Residential solar growing at **~30% YoY** in Bangalore due to:
  - High electricity tariffs (₹7–₹12/unit for >200 units)
  - MNRE subsidy (up to 40% for residential <3kW, 20% for 3-10kW)
  - Rising awareness of power cuts / load shedding
  - Tech workforce with rooftop ownership

## Key Market Dynamics

### Drivers
1. **Subsidy availability** — MNRE rooftop solar subsidy live and cashless
2. **Net metering** — Karnataka has functional net metering for solar exports
3. **High grid tariff** — Makes solar payback attractive (3–5 years)
4. **Corporate sustainability** — ESG mandates driving commercial/industrial adoption
5. **Apartment boom** — RWAs increasingly saying yes to solar

### Barriers
1. **Upfront cost perception** — Despite subsidy, ₹1.5–3 lakh for residential feels high
2. **Roof ownership ambiguity** — Tenant vs owner confusion
3. **Trust deficit** — Many fly-by-night operators post 2018 solar boom
4. **AMC neglect** — Panels degrade, inverter fails, no maintenance
5. **Dispute over net metering** — BESCOM delays and disputes

## Seasonal Patterns

- **Peak inquiry:** January–May (pre-monsoon, highest electricity bills)
- **Installations peak:** March–July (delivery after inquiry spike)
- **Low season:** Monsoon (June–September) — fewer leads but less competitive
