## What the Ratings Reveal

### 5-Star Strategy: Orange Energy
**How to achieve 5.0:**
1. **Every customer must be asked for a review** — most companies don't ask
2. **Respond to every review** — even negative ones (shows you care)
3. **Solve complaints publicly** — if someone complains online, fix it fast and update
4. **Photos in reviews** — ask customers to post photos of their installation
5. **Multiple platforms** — Google, Facebook, Justdial, IndiaMART — all matter

### Why Power Planet & Kamal Solar Have 4.9
- Slightly smaller review volume = rating less stable
- 4.9 vs 5.0 — likely one or two 4-star reviews that weren't addressed

### Why SunTrop is 4.7
- Suggests either:
  - Lower installation quality
  - Less responsive to complaints
  - Post-installation support issues
- This is the "good but not great" zone — fixable with better service recovery

## Review Patterns That Kill Ratings

### What Customers Complain About (Industry Pattern):
1. **"Company disappeared after installation"** — No post-installation contact
2. **"Panels not generating what was promised"** — Expectation mismatch
3. **"Inverter keeps tripping"** — Quality or installation issue
4. **"Net metering took 3 months"** — BESCOM issue blamed on company
5. **"Installation took 2 months"** — Timeline over-promise
6. **"Nobody came for AMC"** — Service neglect

### What Customers Praise (Industry Pattern):
1. **"Team was professional and clean"** — Installation discipline
2. **"Explained everything before starting"** — Pre-sale education
3. **"Got my first electricity bill with solar credit"** — Good net metering execution
4. **"Phone was answered even on Sunday"** — Responsive
5. **"System works exactly as shown"** — Expectation management
