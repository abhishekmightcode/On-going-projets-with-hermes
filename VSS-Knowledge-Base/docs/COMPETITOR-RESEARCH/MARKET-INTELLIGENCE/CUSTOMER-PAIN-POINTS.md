# Customer Pain Points in Bangalore Solar Market
**Source:** Competitor analysis + general solar industry research + review patterns

---

## Top Customer Pain Points

### 1. 🕐 Installation Delays
**Problem:** Companies promise fast installation but miss timelines by weeks/months
**Typical timeline promised:** 15–30 days
**Reality:** Often 45–90 days due to material unavailability, labor shortages, net metering delays
**Impact:** Customer frustration, especially if BESCOM approval is slow

**How top competitors address it:**
- ZunSolar: "50-day commission guarantee" with compensation if missed
- Most others: No guarantee — just promises

---

### 2. 💰 Price Confusion / Hidden Costs
**Problem:** Initial quote looks attractive but final bill has:
- Extra charges for civil work
- Higher wiring/electrical upgrade costs
- Net metering application fees
- Transportation / logistics charges
- Site visit charges for "detailed assessment"

**Industry benchmark confusion:**
- Some quote ₹45,000/kW, others ₹65,000/kW
- Customers can't compare without knowing Tier-1 vs Tier-B panels
- Subsidy amount clarity is often absent in first quote

---

### 3. 📋 Net Metering Hassles with BESCOM
**Problem:**
- BESCOM net metering process is bureaucratic (separate form, KYC, inspection)
- Many customers don't understand net metering until after installation
- Delay in net metering = customer pays full bill until meter is approved
- TANGEDCO (Tamil Nadu) vs BESCOM (Karnataka) — Karnataka is slightly better but still slow

**Timeline for net metering:** 15–45 days after installation

---

### 4. 🔧 Quality of Components
**Problem:** Customer receives:
- Tier-B solar panels (lower efficiency, less durability)
- Lower-capacity inverter (underrated)
- Thinner mounting structures
- Sub-standard cables

**How customers find out:** 2–3 years later when generation drops

**Trust signal:** MNRE approved list vs non-approved; company reputation

---

### 5. 🏚️ Post-Installation Support / AMC Neglect
**Problem:**
- Company disappears after installation
- Inverter problems not addressed
- Panel cleaning never offered or done
- AMC offered but renewal is confusing
- No proactive monitoring — customer discovers generation drop months later

**This is the biggest reason for negative reviews**

---

### 6. 📉 Lower-Than-Advertised Generation
**Problem:** Customer expected 5 kW system to generate X units based on company's claim. Reality:
- Shading issues not assessed properly
- Wrong panel orientation
- Poor angle calculation
- Seasonal variation
- Warranty claim process unclear

---

### 7. 🏢 Roof Ownership / RWA Permissions
**Problem:**
- Apartment owner wants solar but RWA says no
- Tenant wants solar but owner is skeptical
- Society approval needed but process unclear
- Multiple owners complicate decisions

**Market gap:** Very few companies actively help with RWA persuasion/negotiation

---

### 8. 🔄 Company Credibility
**Problem:** Many fly-by-night companies opened in 2018 solar boom and closed by 2021
- Customer fears paying ₹2 lakh and company vanishing
- "Who will honor the 25-year warranty?"
- How to trust a local company's longevity?

**Trust signals that work:**
- MNRE empanelment
- CII / ISO certification
- Bank association / financial institution reference
- Long operating history
- Physical office address verification
- Video testimonials from named customers

---

### 9. 💳 Financing Confusion
**Problem:**
- "0% EMI" sounds good but:
  - Processing fee exists
  - Tenure is short (12 months)
  - Requires CIBIL check
  - Loan amount caps don't match system cost
- Subsidy is upfront but requires: application → inspection → approval → disbursement (can take 3–6 months)

**Market gap:** Companies that handle subsidy paperwork reduce customer friction significantly

---

### 10. 📱 Technology Confusion
**Problem:**
- On-grid vs Off-grid vs Hybrid — what do I need?
- String inverter vs Microinverter — what's better for my roof?
- Single-phase vs Three-phase connection
- Battery backup cost vs benefit

**Company failure:** Most companies don't educate before selling. Customer regrets after understanding.
